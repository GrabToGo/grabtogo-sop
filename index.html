Don't have access yet? — How to request your Field Agent account

There is no self-registration. You must request access from your admin — here's exactly how.

Field agent accounts are created by the GrabToGo admin team — you cannot sign up on your own. If you are a new agent and have not received your login credentials yet, follow these steps to request access.

Step A — Contact your admin to request access
1
Reach out to your GrabToGo admin via WhatsApp, phone, or email and send them the following details: • Your full name — as it should appear on the app
• Your email address — the one you want to use to log in (use a personal or work email you check regularly)
• Your preferred Employee ID — e.g. AGT-001 (optional, admin can assign one)
2
Ask the admin to send you an invite on the GrabToGo system. You do not need to know anything about how the admin panel works — just share your details and wait.
3
The admin will process your request and send you an invitation email from GrabToGo. This usually arrives within a few hours.
Step B — Set up your account from the invitation email
4
Open the invitation email from GrabToGo in your inbox. Check your Spam or Promotions folder if you don't see it in your inbox within a few hours.
5
Tap the "Accept Invitation" or "Set up my account" link in the email. This opens the GrabToGo app (or prompts you to download it first if not installed).
6
Create your password when prompted. Choose something secure — minimum 8 characters with a mix of letters and numbers. Save it somewhere safe.
7
Your account is now active. Open the GrabToGo app, enter your email and password on the login screen, and tap Sign In. The app will automatically take you to the Field Agent Dashboard.
⚠
Invitation link expires in 24 hours. If you tap the link after it has expired, you will see an error. Contact your admin and ask them to resend the invite — they can do this in a few seconds.
ℹ
The GrabToGo app is the same one used by customers and vendors, but the app recognises your role and takes you directly to the Field Agent Dashboard — not the customer home screen. If you land on the wrong screen, you may have logged in with the wrong email. Sign out and try again.
✓
Already have your credentials? Skip straight to Step 1 — you only need to follow Step 0 once when setting up for the first time.

Step 2 of 4 — Pin Shop Location on Map

Drop a pin on the exact location of the shop using the live map — done while standing at the shop

1
The map screen opens. The app requests location permission if not already granted — tap Allow While Using App.
2
The map auto-centers on your current GPS location. Since you are doing this on-site at the shop, the map should already be near the correct location. If GPS is denied or unavailable, the map defaults to the Kerala region center. You will need to pan and zoom manually to find the shop.
3
Move the pin to the exact shop entrance by dragging the map — the pin stays in the center. The address below the map updates automatically via reverse geocoding as you move.
4
Use the 📍 recenter button (navigation arrow icon) to snap back to your GPS location if you've scrolled away.
5
Once the pin is accurately placed on the shop, tap "Next: Upload Documents". The coordinates are saved to the draft.
⚠
Accuracy matters: The pin location is shown to customers on the map view. Place it precisely at the shop entrance — not on the road or a nearby building. Zoom in to at least street level before confirming.
✓
Resuming: If you previously saved a location for this draft, it will be pre-loaded when you return to Step 2. You can still adjust it if needed.

Payment Collection Detail — QR Code, Share Link & Manual Verify

Detailed guide for all three payment methods used within Step 4 Review & Submit

The Registration Fee section on the Review screen has three payment collection methods. Use the best one for the situation.

MethodWhen to UseHow It Works
📱 QR Code
Recommended		 Vendor has UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) on their phone Tap "Generate Payment QR Code". A QR appears. The vendor scans it with their UPI app and pays ₹353. App auto-detects payment within 5 seconds.
🔗 Share Payment Link Vendor wants to pay on their own device via browser After generating the QR, tap "Share Payment Link". A WhatsApp / SMS share sheet opens with the payment URL. Send to vendor's number.
✏ Manual Verify Auto-detection fails after 60 seconds After 60s, a text field appears. Enter the Razorpay Payment ID or UPI transaction reference number from the vendor's payment confirmation. Tap "Verify Payment".
1
Tap "Generate Payment QR Code" (red button). A loading spinner appears briefly while the Razorpay payment link is created.
2
A QR code appears on screen showing the amount (₹353). Ask the vendor to open their UPI app and scan the QR code.
3
A "Waiting for payment…" spinner appears below the QR. The app polls Razorpay every 5 seconds automatically. Do not navigate away from the screen.
4
When the vendor completes payment, the app shows a green "✓ ₹353 Payment Received!" toast and the QR is replaced with a green confirmation badge showing the payment reference ID.
5
If 60 seconds pass with no detection: A "Verify Payment Manually" field appears. Ask the vendor for the UPI transaction reference or Razorpay Payment ID from their payment app. Enter it and tap "Verify Payment".
6
If polling stops after 5 minutes: A "Resume Checking" button appears. Tap it to restart the auto-detection, or use manual verify.
⚠
Offline / poor signal: If you get a "Failed to create payment link" error, check your internet connection and tap Retry (the button text changes to "Retry"). Move to a better signal area and try again. The draft is still saved.
✓
No cash accepted: The app only tracks digital payments via Razorpay. All payments must go through the QR or payment link — do not collect cash and mark manually unless using the UPI reference fallback.

Vendor Login — Help the Shop Owner Sign In for the First Time

The vendor's account is now live — help them download the app and log in as a vendor

The vendor was registered using their email and a password set during the onboarding wizard. Help them access their account on their own phone.

1
Ask the vendor to download the GrabToGo app on their phone from the App Store (iPhone) or Play Store (Android) if they haven't already. Share the download link via WhatsApp to make it easy.
2
Once installed, open the app. On the Login screen, scroll to the bottom and tap "Want to sell? Register as Vendor" — then tap the "Already have an account? Sign In" link, or simply go back to the main login screen.
3
Tap "Vendor Sign In" (separate from the customer login). Enter the vendor's email address and the password they set during registration. If the vendor forgot their password, tap "Forgot Password?" to receive a reset link on their email.
4
On successful login, the app takes them directly to the Vendor Dashboard. Show them the key sections: Offers, Stories, Reviews, and Settings.
5
Point out the verification status banner at the top of the dashboard — it should now show a green "Verified ✓" badge confirming their shop is live on GrabToGo.
⚠
If the vendor lands on the customer home screen: they logged in as a customer, not a vendor. Sign out and use the Vendor Sign In link specifically. The role is tied to which login entry point is used.
✓
Save the password: Strongly advise the vendor to save their password in their phone's password manager or note it down securely. Resetting via email later can be confusing for less tech-savvy users.

Shop Setup — Profile, Hours & Photos

Help the vendor complete their shop profile before creating any offers — a complete profile builds customer trust

From the Vendor Dashboard, navigate to Settings → Shop Settings. Walk the vendor through completing each section.

1
Shop Logo / Cover Photo: Tap the camera icon on the shop profile image. Upload a clear logo or shop photo. This appears at the top of the shop page that customers see. A photo from outside the shop with the signboard visible works great as a cover photo.
2
Shop Description: A short text (max 200 characters) describing what the shop sells. Help the vendor write something clear and friendly — e.g. "Fresh homemade sweets and snacks. Order online or walk in. Special offers daily!"
3
Phone Number: Confirm the contact number is correct. This is displayed to customers with a tap-to-call button on the shop page.
4
Operating Hours: Tap "Edit Hours" and set the opening and closing time for each day of the week. Mark rest days (e.g. Sunday closed). This shows customers when the shop is open.
5
Shop Location: Verify the map pin is correctly placed on the shop. If it looks off, the vendor can adjust it from Shop Settings → Location.
✓
Why this matters: Shops with a complete profile (photo + description + hours) get significantly more taps from customers than shops with blank profiles. Spend 5 minutes here — it directly affects how many customers visit the shop.

Creating the First Offer Listing

Help the vendor publish their first offer — this is the most important step to get them active on the platform immediately

From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the red "+ Add Offer" button or go to the Offers tab. Walk the vendor through creating their very first offer together.

1
Offer Image (required): Tap the camera placeholder. Ask the vendor to take a photo of their product or choose one from their gallery. The image must be in a 4:3 ratio. A clear, well-lit photo on a clean surface works best. Help them take one right there if they don't have a suitable image.
2
Offer Title (required, max 100 chars): A short punchy title. Help the vendor phrase it well — e.g. "50% off on all Biriyani orders today!" rather than just "offer on food".
3
Description (optional, max 500 chars): More details about the offer — conditions, timings, contact. Encourage them to fill this in even briefly.
4
Original Price & Discounted Price: Enter both prices. The app automatically calculates and displays the discount percentage to customers (e.g. 43% OFF).
5
Validity Period: Set the start date (defaults to today) and end date. Recommend 7–30 days for the first offer so it gets enough visibility. Short offers (1–2 days) expire before enough customers see them. 30 days is ideal for a first offer.
6
Tap "Create Offer". The offer goes live immediately and appears on the GrabToGo home feed for customers nearby. Show the vendor their live offer card by opening the app as a customer.
✓
Show them the result: After the offer is created, open the customer view of the app and show the vendor their offer appearing in the feed. This is a powerful moment — seeing their shop live on the app for the first time builds confidence and excitement.
ℹ
Plan limits: The number of active offers a vendor can have depends on their subscription plan. On the free trial, they typically get access to all features. Encourage them to create at least 2–3 offers during the trial period so they experience full value before subscribing.

Stories, Flyers & Promotions — Optional but Powerful

Briefly introduce the vendor to the other content tools available on the platform

After creating their first offer, give the vendor a quick tour of the other content features. You don't need to set everything up — the goal is to show them what's possible so they use the app independently.

FeatureWhat It DoesWhat to Tell the Vendor
📸 Stories Short image or video posts that appear as circular avatars on the customer home screen — like Instagram Stories. Expire after 24 hours. "Post a story whenever you have something to share — a new item, a festival special, or just a photo of your shop. Customers nearby will see it instantly."
📰 Flyers Promotional flyer images that appear in a carousel on the customer home screen. More permanent than stories. "Create a flyer for a big sale or seasonal promotion — it stays visible to customers longer than a story."
🚀 Boost / Promote Pay to have an offer appear at the top of the feed for more customers. Available as Starter (3 days), Boost (7 days), or Mega (14 days) packages. "When you have a really important offer, you can pay a small amount to show it to many more customers nearby."
⭐ Reviews Customers can leave star ratings and photos. Vendor can respond to reviews from the Reviews tab. "Ask your regular customers to leave a review — good ratings build trust with new customers."
✓
Keep it simple: Do not overwhelm the vendor with every feature at once. Focus on getting their first offer live (Step 9) and their subscription active (Step 11). Stories and flyers can be covered on a follow-up visit or via a quick WhatsApp tutorial.

Subscription — Mandatory Plan Selection

Every vendor must subscribe to a plan before you leave. This is a required part of your onboarding — do not skip it.

✕
This step is mandatory. Your onboarding is not complete until the vendor has selected and paid for a subscription plan. Do not leave the shop without completing this step.

From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the subscription banner or go to Settings → Subscription Plans. Help the vendor choose the right plan for their business.

How to present the plans
1
Open the Subscription Plans screen. Show the vendor the Monthly / Yearly toggle at the top — yearly saves money. Start by showing yearly prices to anchor higher value.
2
Walk through each plan briefly: • Basic — Good for shops just starting. Limited offers, basic analytics.
• Standard — Most popular. More offers, flyers, standard analytics. Recommend this for most vendors.
• Premium — For established businesses. Unlimited offers, advanced analytics, ad campaigns.
3
Recommend Standard for most vendors — it covers everything they need to grow without being overwhelming or expensive. Use this script:

"Most of our shops in Kottayam are on the Standard plan. It gives you unlimited reach and all the tools you need. The Basic plan has limits that most active shops hit quickly. I'd recommend Standard to get the full benefit."
4
Once the vendor chooses a plan, tap "Choose Plan". The checkout screen shows the price breakdown including 18% GST. Confirm the details are correct.
5
Tap "Pay via Razorpay". The vendor completes payment using UPI, debit/credit card, or net banking. On success, the dashboard updates immediately with the plan badge.
6
Confirm the subscription is active — the dashboard should show the plan name and renewal date. Take a screenshot as proof of completed onboarding.
Handling objections
Vendor saysYour response
"Let me think about it""I understand. But your shop is already live right now — customers can see it. Without a plan, you won't be able to post offers or show up prominently. The trial ends soon and a plan keeps you active."
"It's too expensive""The Basic plan starts at just ₹X per month — that's less than ₹X per day. One extra customer a week from GrabToGo more than covers it."
"Can I do it later?""Of course, but I'd recommend doing it now while I'm here to help you set it up. It only takes 2 minutes and you get access to everything immediately."
"I'll try the free trial first""Great — the trial is already active. Once it ends, a plan keeps all your offers and data. Let's get it set up now so you're not interrupted."
⚠
If the vendor absolutely refuses to subscribe today: Do not force it. Note it in your onboarding record, inform your admin, and schedule a follow-up within 48 hours. The vendor's trial period gives a natural deadline — use it as a reason to follow up.

Complete Field Agent Onboarding Checklist
Use this for every vendor. Both Part 1 and Part 2 must be completed for a full onboarding.
Part 1 — Registration & Submission
  • You have a field agent account — if not, contact your admin with your full name and email to request an invite
  • Phone has internet connection, GPS enabled, and camera permission granted to GrabToGo
  • Logged in to the app — Field Agent Dashboard is showing (not customer home)
  • Tapped "+ New Onboarding" or resumed an existing draft
  • Step 1: Vendor name, phone, shop name, category, address, and city all filled and validated
  • Step 2: Map pin placed on the exact shop entrance (zoomed in to street level)
  • Step 3: Shop Front Photo uploaded — green checkmark visible
  • Step 3: Additional documents uploaded where available (PAN, GST, FSSAI)
  • Step 4: All details reviewed — vendor info, shop, map, and documents correct
  • Step 4: QR code generated and vendor paid ₹353 — green "Payment Collected" badge showing
  • Step 4: "Submit for Review" tapped — onboarding status changed to "Submitted"
  • Vendor informed their application is under review and will be notified within 24–48 hours
Part 2 — Vendor Activation (after approval)
  • Vendor approval confirmed — status shows "Approved" in app or admin has informed you
  • Step 7: Vendor has downloaded GrabToGo app and logged in via Vendor Sign In successfully
  • Step 7: Vendor dashboard visible and "Verified ✓" badge showing
  • Step 8: Shop profile photo uploaded
  • Step 8: Shop description written and operating hours set
  • Step 9: First offer created with image, title, price, and validity date
  • Step 9: Vendor has seen their offer live on the customer feed
  • Step 10: Vendor briefly shown Stories, Flyers, and Promotion features
  • Step 11: Subscription plan selected and payment completed ✅ (Mandatory)
  • Step 11: Dashboard shows active plan name and renewal date — screenshot taken as proof
  • Vendor knows how to create offers and stories independently
  • Vendor has your WhatsApp number for follow-up support
